Stereotypes about Dutch people
that are actually not true

It is important to mention that stereotypes do not accurately represent the entire population. The people of a country are a diverse group of individuals with a wide range of backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives.

Like many cultures, the Dutch have been subject to stereotypes that are not always accurate. Here are a few stereotypes about Dutch people that are actually not true:

All Dutch people are tall:
While the Dutch are known for being a tall people, this stereotype is not entirely true. According to the World Health Organization, the average height for Dutch men is around 5'11" and for Dutch women is around 5'7", which is taller than the average height for other Europeans but not all of them are tall.

All Dutch people are blond and blue-eyed:
While many Dutch people have blonde hair and blue eyes, this stereotype is not entirely accurate. The Dutch population is diverse and includes people from many different ethnic backgrounds, and the population is made up of people with different hair and eye colors.

All Dutch people ride bikes:
While cycling is a popular mode of transportation in the Netherlands, not all Dutch people ride bikes. Many people in the Netherlands also use cars, public transportation, and walk as a mode of transportation.

All Dutch people are liberal:
The Netherlands has a reputation for being a liberal country and is known for being the first country in the world to legalize same-sex marriage. However, not all Dutch people hold liberal views and the country is politically diverse, as in any other country.

It is important to be mindful of stereotypes and not make assumptions about a group of people based on preconceived notions.
Whatever their height, whatever their politics, one thing is for sure: the Dutch are one of the nicest people I have ever met.
