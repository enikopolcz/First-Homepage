A Journey Through My Heart

As a seasoned traveller and someone who has fallen in love with Italy, I am thrilled to share my experiences and insights on this beautiful country. From the first time I set foot on Italian soil, I was struck by the charm and beauty of this incredible place. Over the years, I have had the privilege of visiting many times and have come to know it as a second home.
With its rich history, stunning architecture, delectable cuisine, and vibrant culture, Italy has left a lasting impression on me. From the bustling streets of Rome to the rolling hills of Tuscany, from the stunning coastline of the Amalfi Coast to the tranquil canals of Venice, there is something truly special about this country.

Rome
Rome, the Eternal City

I’ve visited Rome many times, I can say without a doubt that this city never ceases to amaze me. There is always something new to discover in the Eternal City. But where does this name came from? The city was founded in 753 BC and has been a centre of power and influence for thousands of years. From the Roman Empire to the Renaissance, from the Catholic Church to the Italian state, Rome has been a witness to some of the most important events in Western history. Its long and storied past, combined with its continued importance as a cultural, political, and religious centre, has earned it the nickname "Eternal City".

Treasures on top of each other
the fragrance of a city

One of the things that makes Rome truly special to me is its unique fragrance, which seems to change with the seasons. In the springtime, the city comes alive with the sweet scent of blooming flowers and freshly cut grass. This is hands down my favourite time to visit Rome, as the warm sunshine and mild temperatures make it the perfect setting for exploring the city's many treasures.

Some tips I find useful

If you're planning a trip to Rome, here are a few tips to help you make the most of your time in the city:

1. Plan ahead: With so many sights and attractions to see, it's important to plan your itinerary in advance to make sure you don't miss anything.

2. Wear comfortable shoes: Rome is a sprawling city with plenty of hills, cobblestone streets, and uneven surfaces, so be sure to wear comfortable shoes for all that walking!

3. Be mindful of the weather: Rome can be quite warm during the summer months, so be sure to stay hydrated and wear light, breathable clothing.

4. Experience the local cuisine: Rome is famous for its delicious cuisine, so be sure to try some of the local specialties such as pasta alla carbonara and saltimbocca alla romana.

An interesting fact you may not know

One interesting fact about Rome that not many people know is that the city has its own beach. Ostia Lido, located just a few miles from the city centre, is a popular destination for Romans looking to escape the summer heat. With its wide sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lively promenade, it's easy to forget that you're just a stone's throw from one of the world's most famous cities. So, next time you're in Rome, why not take a break from all that sightseeing and enjoy a day by the sea? It's a bit cold, but who cares, you are in Rome!

The Miraculous Cinque Terre of Liguria

I first visited Cinque Terre when I was babysitting between two semesters at university. I want to make one thing clear: the pictures do not lie! This charming stretch of coastline, comprised of five colourful villages perched atop cliffs overlooking the Mediterranean, left me in awe with its special beauty. The name "Cinque Terre" translates to "Five Lands". These villages are so picturesque, it's no wonder they've been named a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Falling in Love with Toscana

None of my destinations have captured my heart quite like Toscana and its beautiful capital, Firenze. From its vineyards to its rich history and art, Toscana offers a truly unique and unforgettable experience. One of the things I love most about Toscana is its cuisine. The region is famous for its delicious and hearty dishes, made from locally grown ingredients and traditional recipes passed down through generations. Whether it's a simple plate of pasta with a fresh tomato sauce or a decadent feast of roasted meats and vegetables, Toscana never fails to satisfy. Of course, the cuisine is just one aspect of what makes Toscana so special. The landscape is another. From the rolling hills of Chianti to the countryside of Val d'Orcia, Toscana offers a stunning tapestry of greens, blues, and golds, it’s no wonder this region is referred to as the "artists' canvas."

Firenze, you fabulous!

Firenze, the capital of Toscana, is also a city of great historical and cultural importance. From its famous museums, such as the Uffizi and the Accademia, to its beautiful churches and palaces, is a city that truly captures the essence of the Renaissance. Strolling through the streets of Firenze I always feel like taking a step back in time, surrounded by the beauty and grandeur of a bygone era. If I had to pick one region in Italy that I could call my favourite, it would be quite impossible. However, Toscana holds a special place in my heart, thanks to its atmosphere. Whether you're a history buff, an art lover, a foodie, or simply someone in search of a unique and unforgettable experience, you have to feel it with your own senses, because I try my best, but I can't convey the feeling you'll get there.
