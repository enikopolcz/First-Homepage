the netherlands

The Enchanted Home of Neverland

In 2018 spring I moved to the Netherlands, and I was blown away by the country's commitment to sustainability, rich culture, and impeccable tidiness. During my stay, I visited Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Arnhem, and other smaller towns, each offering its own unique perspective on what makes the Netherlands such a special place.
One of the first things that caught my eye was the country's impressive sustainability efforts. The Netherlands is a world leader in sustainable practices, with a strong focus on renewable energy, clean water management, and reducing waste.
In fact, the country aims to become fully carbon neutral by 2050, and it is well on its way to reaching this goal. The widespread use of bikes and the development of bike lanes make it easier for people to get around while reducing their carbon footprint. Windmills, a symbol of Dutch heritage, are also a significant source of renewable energy in the country.

Stunning Sustainability
a place where everything is exciting

Culture is another aspect that I found fascinating in the Netherlands. From the famous tulip gardens to the famous works of Vincent van Gogh, there is no shortage of cultural experiences in this country. Dutch cuisine, with its focus on seasonal ingredients and fresh flavours, is also a highlight of the country's cultural scene. The Dutch are proud of their cultural heritage, and it is reflected in their museums, festivals, and daily life.
Whether you're interested in music, art, food, or just soaking up the local culture, there's always something happening in this vibrant country.

Art in every corner

My all-time favourite celebration was undoubtedly King's Day (Koningsdag). Held annually on April 27th to mark the birthday of the current king, King's Day is a nationwide celebration filled with street parties, music, and other festivities. I arrived just in time for the festivities to begin and was immediately struck by the energy and excitement in the air. The city was awash with orange, the national colour of the Netherlands, as people of all ages took to the streets to celebrate. One of the highlights of King's Day for me was the street market, where vendors sold everything from food and drinks to handmade crafts and vintage clothing. I was amazed at the variety of items on offer and the lively atmosphere of the market. Another highlight was the music, which was everywhere I turned. From street performers to DJ stages, the sounds of King's Day filled the air and kept the party going all day long. I especially enjoyed the live bands, which played everything from traditional Dutch folk music to contemporary pop and rock.

the tidiest country i've seen so far

The tidiness of the Netherlands was something that I was not expecting. Everywhere I went, I saw well-maintained streets, tidy parks, and clean waterways. The Dutch have a strong sense of community, and it shows in their efforts to keep their cities and towns clean and beautiful. It was a pleasure to walk around and take in the sights and sounds of the country, and I couldn't help but feel a sense of pride in the way the Dutch care for their environment.

the North Sea

The Netherlands borders the North Sea, which could be a little bit different “sea-experience” when you get there. It is obviously colder considering that is located in the North Atlantic Ocean, at a high latitude, which means that it is far from the equator and is exposed to cold, Arctic air masses. Additionally, the North Atlantic Drift, a warm ocean current that originates in the Gulf of Mexico, does not reach the North Sea. Even though it may not have the average warmth of other seas, it can still be a rewarding and memorable experience.

The North Sea in August
"The Two-Faced Charm"

As I traveled through the cities and countryside of the Netherlands, I was struck by the unique way that modern and traditional buildings seem to coexist side by side. Whether you're in the bustling city of Amsterdam or a quaint village in the countryside, you'll see examples of both contemporary and historical architecture, each adding its own character and charm to the landscape. In the heart of Amsterdam, you'll find sleek, modern skyscrapers towering over quaint, centuries-old canal houses. Yet, despite their stark differences, both types of buildings seem to fit together seamlessly, creating a diverse and dynamic cityscape. Similarly, in the countryside, you'll find traditional thatched-roof farmhouses sitting next to sleek, modern wind turbines. The contrast between the two types of structures is striking, yet they seem to complement each other, creating a beautiful and harmonious landscape. It's a fascinating example of how the Netherlands has managed to embrace both its rich history and its forward-thinking, innovative spirit. This coexistence of modern and traditional architecture is a testament to the country's willingness to look towards the future while still honoring its past.

Double perspective
Modern masterpieces

One city that particularly stood out to me was Rotterdam, which is a prime example of the country's innovative spirit. From its cutting-edge architecture to its thriving port and thriving cultural scene, Rotterdam is a city that is constantly evolving and pushing the boundaries of what is possible. The city is home to some of the most impressive examples of modern architecture in the world, including the iconic Cube Houses and the Erasmus Bridge. In addition to its stunning architecture, Rotterdam is also home to a vibrant cultural scene, with world-class museums, theaters, and galleries. The city is also a hub for business and innovation, with a thriving port that is the gateway to Europe for many international companies.

My favourite city: Rotterdam
Is this country a must-see?

In conclusion, my trip to the Netherlands was an eye-opening experience. It's not only a must-see, but a must-stay! From its impressive sustainability efforts to its rich culture and impeccable tidiness, the country has left a lasting impression on me. Whether I was exploring the bustling streets of Amsterdam, admiring the modern architecture of Rotterdam, or taking in the beauty of the tulip gardens in Arnhem, I was continually amazed by what I saw. If you're looking for a destination that is both environmentally conscious and culturally rich, the Netherlands is a must-visit.
